Brand overview

Who we are

The story, the mission, and the people we build for.

Vistrow Voice is an AI voice agent platform. Businesses connect it to their phone lines and websites, and it answers every call or chat instantly, in the caller's own language, qualifies the lead the way a trained salesperson would, and books the appointment before the conversation ends.

We exist because the moment a lead goes cold is almost always the moment nobody picked up. A missed call at 9pm, a website visitor who won't wait on hold, a regional-language caller a call centre script can't serve well — each one is revenue a growing business can't afford to lose, and can't afford to staff for around the clock.

Vistrow Voice is that always-on team member: patient, fluent, consistent, and never off shift.

Who we serve

Sales-driven Indian businesses with real call and lead volume — real estate developers, clinics, consultancies, and service businesses running ads or a busy front desk — who need every inbound lead handled the moment it arrives, in Hindi, English, or any of the languages their customers actually speak.

What we're not

A chatbot bolted onto a website. A call centre you have to manage. A tool that only works in English.

What we are

A voice-first, multilingual teammate that qualifies, books, and hands off — built for how Indian businesses actually sell.

02

Brand personality

How we come across

Five traits that should be true of every touchpoint — the product, the website, the agent's own voice on a call.

Fluent

Comfortable switching language mid-sentence, the way a good Indian salesperson naturally does. Never stilted, never translated-sounding.

Composed

Calm under a full call queue. Doesn't rush the customer, doesn't sound like it's reading a script.

Attentive

Remembers what the caller just said. Asks one relevant question at a time, not a form read aloud.

Forward

Modern and capable without being cold or over-engineered. Confidence, not jargon.

Dependable

Answers every time, logs every call, never drops a lead. The trait the other four exist to support.

Voice and tone

ContextWe sound like
Marketing & websiteDirect and confident. Plain language over feature lists — say what it does, not what it "leverages" or "empowers."
Product UIEfficient and calm. Short labels, sentence case, no exclamation marks, no "please" on system copy.
The agent, on a real callWarm, patient, human-paced. Never robotic phrasing, never a wall of information at once.
Errors & failuresPlain about what happened and what to do next. No apologising, no hiding behind vague error codes.

Never

Name the underlying AI models, speech engines, or infrastructure vendors in anything customer-facing. The technology stack changes; "Vistrow Voice" is the only name that should ever reach a customer's ears or eyes.

Logomark

Logo direction

There is no final mark yet — this is the creative brief to generate and evaluate one against.

The mark should read as voice made visible — the moment sound becomes understanding. It needs to work as a tiny app icon and favicon first, and scale up from there — not the other way round.

Direction A

Waveform pulse

An abstract soundwave — a few bars or a single continuous line — with one bar or peak breaking rhythm to suggest a spoken word landing. Should feel alive, not decorative.

Direction B

The listening V

A geometric "V" (for Vistrow) built from two soundwave strokes meeting at a point — doubles as a wordmark-free icon and a monogram.

Direction C

Open channel

A rounded, open bracket or speech-bubble form with a single soundwave line passing through it — "the call that's always open."

Clear space & minimum size

clear space = mark height, on all sides

Minimum size: 24px for digital (favicon/app icon), 12mm for print. Below that, drop to the wordmark alone.

Do

  • Reduce cleanly to one flat colour
  • Work in reverse (light mark on dark, dark on light)
  • Stay legible at 24px
  • Keep it wordless-readable — the mark alone should say "Vistrow"

Don't

  • Use a literal telephone handset or headset icon
  • Use a generic "AI circuit brain" motif
  • Rely on gradients to read correctly
  • Add fine detail that breaks up at small sizes

Rules

Dark mode is not light mode inverted — text and accent colours are re-picked for contrast in each mode, never auto-inverted. Every colour choice must pass a plain test: would this still be readable if the background were near-black? If not, it needs a dedicated dark-mode value, defined here, not guessed at build time.

Product UI defaults to dark mode. Marketing site, documents, and printed materials default to light mode.

Product & UI

How the brand behaves in product

The dashboard, widget, and every screen a customer touches follow the same rules as the brand itself.

Cards, not tables-by-default

Structured data lives in cards with a 12px radius and a hairline border. Tables appear only where rows are genuinely comparable, and collapse to stacked cards on mobile.

Status is colour + shape

Never colour alone — a status always pairs a coloured dot or pill with a plain-language label, so it still reads correctly for colour-blind users.

Icons: outline, one family

Material Symbols, outlined weight only, throughout the product. No mixed icon sets, no filled icons except for the rare "currently active" state.

Flat surfaces, restrained accents

No decorative gradients or glow effects on structural UI. Motion and glow are reserved for a small number of deliberate, meaningful moments — a live call indicator, a launcher bubble.
